How to meet the fund outcomes

Projects applying for funding will have to show (with evidence) how they will meet a common set of programme outcomes.

What are the outcomes

All projects MUST meet the following outcome:

  • That your coastal community will benefit and are able to use their assets (physical, natural, environmental, social, economic and/or cultural) better while promoting sustainable economic growth and jobs.

All projects MUST MEET AT LEAST ONE of the following outcomes:

  • Deliver greater capacity to create a sustainable economic future where the coastal community is better equipped to adapt to change
  • Demonstrate partnerships - within a community and/or across coastal communities - which support economic innovation, enterprise and investment
  • Demonstrate that people in the community have more opportunities for training and skill development, including volunteering

Criteria checklist

Expanding on the outcomes you need to meet, proposals MUST demonstrate how they meet the following criteria:

  • Encourage sustainable economic growth. You must show how you proposal encourages economic growth in the area. Benefits will not have to be delivered within time limit, but you will have to define how and when they will be delivered. This provides assurance of the projects viability while allowing for medium / long term planning.
  • Demonstrate that the proposal fits with the broad economic priorities of the local area. Where possible, link this to your wider economic vision including private sector commitment and community support.
  • Demonstrate how the proposal unlocks barriers, addresses a need or opportunity within the area. Provide clear evidence of the barrier currently faced and how it will be overcome to provide benefit to the community.
  • Demonstrate (where appropriate) how your proposal contributes to green economic growth. Provide clear evidence of the economic benefit and its environmental focus.
  • Explain how you plan to work with other keys partners to deliver outcomes. This could be with public, private, voluntary, social enterprise or community sectors.
  • Where proposals involve flood and coastal erosion risk management include evidence that you are supported by either the Coastal Protection Authority (erosion), or the Environment Agency (flooding).

You must also demonstrate how Coastal Communities Fund support is vital to this projects success.

Additional evidence

You should also look to include information on:

  • Risk. Be clear on the risks to your project you’ve already identified and how this risk will be managed.
  • Previous success. Where applicable, demonstrate your proven ability to manage and deliver projects, particularly if they involved infrastructure improvements
  • Match funding. If your bid includes match-funding, show how you’ve stress tested it against rules governing other funding sources.
